Ions

An ion is an atom or a group of atoms that has one or more positive or negative electrical charges. Positively charged ions are called cations; negatively charged ions, anions. Ions are formed by the addition of electrons to, or the removal of electrons from, neutral atoms or molecules or other ions; by combination of ions with other particles; or by rupture of a covalent bond between two atoms in such a way that both of the electrons of the bond are left in association with one of the formerly bonded atoms.

Ionic Bonding

An ionic bond, also known as electrovalent bond, is a type of linkage formed from the electrostatic attraction between oppositely charged ions in a chemical compound.

Polyatomic Ions

A polyatomic ion, also known as a molecular ion, is a covalently bonded set of two or more atoms, or of a metal complex, that can be considered to behave as a single unit and that has a net charge that is not zero. Polyatomic ions exist everywhere. Bicarbonate ions help maintain the pH level of our blood. Phosphates are important in multiple metabolic processes.
